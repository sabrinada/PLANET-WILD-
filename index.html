Explore Species

Our planet is home to millions of species, each playing a unique role in maintaining the balance of nature. Explore some of the most fascinating species here!

African Elephant

Explore More

The African elephant is the largest land mammal on Earth, known for its intelligence and strong social bonds. Unfortunately, they face the threat of poaching for their ivory tusks.

Bengal Tiger

Explore More

The Bengal tiger is one of the most endangered species in the world, with only a few thousand individuals left in the wild. Habitat loss and poaching are major threats to their survival.

Polar Bear

Explore More

Polar bears are iconic creatures of the Arctic, but they are rapidly losing their habitat due to climate change and the melting of sea ice.

Amur Leopard

Explore More

Native to the Russian Far East and northeast China, this big cat is critically endangered due to habitat loss and poaching.

Giant Panda

Explore More

Giant pandas are native to China and are famous for their distinct black and white fur. They are an endangered species primarily due to habitat destruction.

Kangaroo

Explore More

Kangaroos are iconic marsupials native to Australia. They are facing habitat destruction, with their populations affected by human encroachment.

Vaquita

Explore More

The vaquita is a critically endangered porpoise in Mexico's Gulf of California, with fewer than 20 left due to fishing net entanglement.

Javan Rhino

Explore More

The Javan rhino is a critically endangered species found only in Ujung Kulon National Park, Indonesia. Fewer than 80 remain due to habitat loss and poaching.

Sumatra Orang Utan

Explore More

The Sumatran orangutan is a critically endangered great ape found only on Sumatra, Indonesia. Habitat loss and poaching threaten its survival.

Mountain Gorilla

Explore More

The mountain gorilla is a critically endangered primate found in the forests of central Africa. Conservation efforts have increased their numbers, but habitat loss and poaching remain threats.

Saola

Explore More

The saola, or "Asian unicorn," is a critically endangered and elusive mammal found in the Annamite Mountains of Laos and Vietnam. Habitat loss and poaching threaten its survival.

Kakapo

Explore More

The kakapo is a critically endangered, flightless parrot native to New Zealand. It is nocturnal and threatened by habitat loss and introduced predators. Conservation efforts have boosted its population.
